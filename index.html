About

Systems that hold up under real interaction.

I've spent the last five-plus years building real-time 3D systems for training, visualization, and play — work that has to survive contact with an actual user, not just a demo reel.

That's ranged from industrial training simulators wired up to real hardware over serial, to product visualization builds, WebXR experiences that run straight in the browser, AR overlays, cognitive training games, and custom shader work when a scene needs a specific look and can't afford the performance hit to get it.

I care about the mechanic being physically believable first — the polish comes after the thing actually behaves the way it should.
